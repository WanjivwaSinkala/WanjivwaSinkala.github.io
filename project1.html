USAID Alternatives to Charcoal (A2C) Project Evaluation

As part of my Postgraduate Diploma in Monitoring & Evaluation Methods (2024/25), I conducted a process evaluation of the USAID-funded Alternatives to Charcoal (A2C) project (2021–2026). The project aims to reduce deforestation in Lusaka by promoting the adoption of Alternative Technologies and Fuels (ATFs), such as improved cookstoves and renewable energy solutions.

Key Contributions

  • Assessed project implementation processes, focusing on market barriers and adoption of ATFs.
  • Reviewed the design and delivery of Social Behaviour Change Communication (SBCC) interventions.
  • Applied participatory evaluation methods including workshops, meetings, and surveys.
  • Identified challenges such as limited resources and audience resistance, highlighting the need for stronger stakeholder engagement and capacity building.

Outcome

The evaluation emphasized the importance of multi-stakeholder collaboration, tailored communication strategies, and integrated livelihood approaches to scale ATF adoption. These insights inform future programming and contribute to Zambia’s environmental conservation and clean energy transition goals.
