USAID Alternatives to Charcoal (A2C) Project Evaluation

The USAID Alternatives to Charcoal (A2C) project seeks to reduce deforestation and promote sustainable energy practices in Lusaka, Zambia, by encouraging households to adopt Alternative Technologies and Fuels (ATFs), such as improved cookstoves and renewable energy solutions. This evaluation aimed to assess the project’s implementation processes, focusing on identifying and addressing market barriers and evaluating the design and delivery of social behaviour change and communication (SBCC) interventions.

Key findings revealed that participatory methods, including workshops (42.9%), meetings (35.7%), and surveys (21.4%), were central to identifying market barriers. However, challenges such as limited resources and logistical constraints were significant, affecting timelines and implementation strategies. To mitigate these, stakeholder engagement and capacity-building initiatives were highlighted as critical solutions.

SBCC interventions were designed using participatory approaches and delivered primarily through community meetings (70%), with social media (50%) and workshops (40%) playing complementary roles. Despite their overall effectiveness, challenges such as resource constraints and resistance from target audiences were noted, emphasizing the need to address barriers to facilitate broader adoption of ATFs.

The findings show the importance of multi-stakeholder collaboration, robust community engagement, and tailored communication strategies in overcoming barriers to ATF adoption. These insights have implications for future programming, emphasizing the need for integrated approaches that combine market interventions with alternative livelihood strategies to reduce charcoal production sustainably. The lessons learned provide a foundation for scaling and refining interventions, contributing to Zambia’s environmental conservation and energy transition goals.
