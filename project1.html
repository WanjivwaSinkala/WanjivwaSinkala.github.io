USAID Alternatives to Charcoal (A2C) Project Evaluation

The USAID-funded Alternatives to Charcoal (A2C) project (2021–2026) is designed to reduce deforestation and greenhouse gas emissions by promoting sustainable energy solutions in Lusaka and surrounding areas.

My role focused on conducting a process evaluation to assess project design, implementation strategies, and adoption levels of sustainable energy technologies. The evaluation provided insights into both the successes and operational challenges of A2C, informing recommendations for improved project delivery and long-term sustainability.

Key areas of assessment included:

  • Promotion of alternative energy sources (e.g., LPG, biomass briquettes).
  • Community awareness and behavioral change initiatives.
  • Impact on deforestation rates and household energy usage.
  • Collaboration with private sector and local authorities.
